How It Works

Four steps to a better morning.

  1. 01

    Set your alarm

    Pick a time, choose which days it repeats, select your ringtone, and dial in your push-up target (5–100).

  2. 02

    Alarm fires

    The ringtone plays at full volume via AVAudioSession — even if your phone was on silent. The only way to stop it is the challenge screen.

  3. 03

    Get to the floor

    Prop your phone 1–2 metres away. The front camera opens and begins detecting your body using Apple's Vision framework. No account, no cloud, no delay.

  4. 04

    Complete your reps → earn silence

    Each full down-then-up cycle counts as one rep. Hit your target and the alarm stops automatically. Your streak gets updated. Go be productive.

Privacy Policy

Your data stays on your device.

Camera Usage

PushAlarm requests access to your device's front-facing camera solely to count push-up repetitions in real time. Camera frames are processed in memory using Apple's on-device Vision framework (VNDetectHumanBodyPoseRequest) and are never stored to disk, never transmitted over the network, and never shared with any third party. The only output that leaves the camera pipeline is a numeric rep count.

Notifications

PushAlarm uses iOS local notifications (UNUserNotificationCenter) to fire alarms at scheduled times. These notifications are scheduled entirely on-device. No notification data is sent to any server.

Alarm & Settings Data

Your alarms, settings, and challenge history are stored locally on your device using the app's sandboxed Application Support directory and UserDefaults. This data is included in standard iCloud Backup if you have iCloud Backup enabled on your device, but is not accessible by us.

Analytics & Advertising

PushAlarm contains no analytics SDK, no advertising SDK, and no crash-reporting SDK that transmits data off-device. The app makes zero outbound network connections during normal operation.

Third Parties

PushAlarm uses no third-party libraries or frameworks. All functionality is implemented using Apple's first-party SDKs (SwiftUI, AVFoundation, Vision, UserNotifications, AVAudioSession).

Children

PushAlarm does not knowingly collect personal information from anyone, including children under 13. Because all data remains on-device and nothing is transmitted, there is no personal data to collect.

Camera Tips

Place your phone 1–2 m away on a stable surface, propped at roughly chest height. Ensure shoulders and elbows are fully in frame. Good lighting helps.

⚡

Common Fixes

  • Alarm silent? Check Camera & Notifications permissions in iOS Settings.
  • Reps not counting? Move back so shoulders and elbows are visible.
  • Slow detection? Ensure good, even lighting.
